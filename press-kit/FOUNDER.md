## Bio (English)

**Zakaria Chohra** is an independent scientist, engineer, and the founder of **ASTRO-SCAN**, a real-time space observatory operated from Tlemcen, Algeria. He built the entire platform solo — backend architecture, AI orchestration, orbital mechanics, frontend, infrastructure, and bilingual content — with the goal of making professional-grade astronomical data accessible to anyone with a browser, regardless of language, geography, or budget.

His work sits at the intersection of **astronomy**, **production software engineering**, and **open science**. ASTRO-SCAN integrates live data from NASA, NOAA, ESA, JPL Horizons, CelesTrak, Harvard MicroObservatory, and N2YO; computes ISS pass predictions via SGP4 propagation; runs an AEGIS AI chatbot orchestrating Anthropic Claude, Google Gemini, Groq, and xAI Grok; and serves the Francophone, Arabic-speaking, and global research communities through a hardened Flask production stack.

Zakaria operates ASTRO-SCAN as an **independent, non-commercial scientific platform**, licensed under CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 for free educational and research use. He welcomes collaboration with universities, space agencies, observatories, and citizen science initiatives worldwide.
